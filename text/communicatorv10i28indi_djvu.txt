FACULTY NOTES 



ieth L. Stevenson, of the Department 
of Chemistry, has been invited to spend his spring 
semester 1980, sabbatical and the following summer 
at the Solar Energy Research Institute, SERI, in 
Golden, Colorado, to do research on the photo- 
chemical conversion of solar energy. SERI, which 
is funded by the Department of Energy, was estab- 
lished by Congress in 1975 to serve as the country's 
premier laboratory in the development of solar 
energy technology. Dr. Stevenson will continue his 
research on the use of copper compounds to generate 
hydrogen and electricity. 

Dr. Marjorie Souers, Assistant Professor of Edu- 
cation was one of 60 professors invited to attend the 
AACTE/NCATE Training Session held in Chicago this 
past March. She then proceeded to Detroit for the 
annual conference of the Association for Supervision 
and Curriculum Development. Dr. Souers and Dr. 
Sandford Reichart. Professor of Education at Case 
Western Reserve University, conducted a one day 
action laboratory on "Basic Education: A Vehicle 
for Assuring Quality Through Equity".
